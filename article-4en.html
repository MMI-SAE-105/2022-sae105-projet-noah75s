The Tomb of the Fireflies

Tomb of the Fireflies (火垂るの墓, Hotaru no haka) is a Japanese animated film by Isao Takahata of Studio Ghibli, released in 1988.


Tomb of the Fireflies (火垂るの墓, Hotaru no haka) is a Japanese animated film by Isao Takahata of Studio Ghibli, released in 1988. It is adapted from Grave of the Fireflies, a semi-autobiographical short story written in 1967 by Akiyuki Nosaka. This film has become a classic of Japanese animation and of animation itself, and is considered one of the greatest masterpieces in the field of animation. Not least because of its darkness and tragic scenario which makes this film unsuitable for a young audience. Synopsis: The story takes place in the summer of 1945, in Japan, during the Second World War.

Seita is a fourteen year old teenager. His younger sister, Setsuko, is four. Their father is a senior officer in the Imperial Japanese Navy who has been in the navy for several years. They live with their mother in the city of Kōbe. However, the American armed forces bombed the port city.

The mother could not escape in time from the huge fire that was about to break out in the city. She is badly burned and dies of her injuries...


As a result, the two children are left to their own devices. After trying in vain to contact their father, they go to live with a distant aunt. This aunt, relatively welcoming at first, gradually treats them as burdens, stealing their food and showing contempt for them.

They then move away from there to a shelter lit by fireflies. Problems follow for them: food runs out and Setsuko falls victim to a severe illness. Seita starts to steal food, but is caught by a farmer who beats him up.

In desperation, he goes to the city to empty his parents' bank account and learns of the surrender of Japan and the destruction of the Japanese navy. Back in the shelter with fresh, quality food, he unfortunately fails to save his younger sister, who is dying a slow, agonizing death.

Finding himself alone without a loving family, the older brother also lets himself die. The spirits of the two children, reunited and peaceful, no longer suffering from anything, then contemplate the city where they grew up.

The script of this film is particularly touching, and it left a deep impression on me. It is full of diverse and varied emotions.
