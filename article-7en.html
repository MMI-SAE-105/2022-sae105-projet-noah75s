The damage to the planet

Environmental degradation is the loss of an environment's ability to meet the social, biological and ecological needs of the planet. Environmental degradation occurs when the earth's natural resources are depleted or damaged and the environment is compromised. The consequences of this degradation are diverse and can include species extinction, loss of biodiversity, loss of air quality, water pollution, soil erosion and an increase in the greenhouse effect. Many of these effects are invisible in the short term, but visible in the long term. As such, current actions can influence future environmental degradation.


The causes that affect environmental degradation are very diverse. Pollution degrades the environment Pollution is a change in the environment caused by the introduction of various substances into the environment that harm the environment. There are different types of pollution, which are divided according to the environment affected and the environment affected. The best-known and best-studied types of pollution are soil pollution, air pollution, water pollution and noise pollution, but there are also lesser-known types of pollution such as optical, visual, thermal, electromagnetic, radioactive or genetic pollution...

There are also some. Another way of classifying pollution is by the degree of propagation of the pollutant. So we have :

Diffusion pollution: Pollutants diffuse into the environment. Acid rain caused by wind action. Environmental destruction by industry Much of the environmental degradation is due to industrial activities that produce goods for society at the expense of the environment, produce pollutants or provide people with things (such as cars) that pollute the environment. Impact of environmental destruction on humans


Environmental degradation can have many effects on people and their health. Some of these are:

- Poverty, particularly in developing countries

- Lack of access to basic resources such as clean water

- Spread of insect pathogens or disease vectors. - Contamination of water by pathogenic micro-organisms or chemical pollutants harmful to human health

- Potential human illnesses or complications related to pollutants, such as respiratory diseases, increased incidence of certain types of cancer

- Release of substances hazardous to health into food, mainly through contamination of water with heavy metals.
