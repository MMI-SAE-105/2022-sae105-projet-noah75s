Breaking Bad

Breaking Bad is an American television series in 62 episodes of 47 minutes, created by Vince Gilligan.


Breaking Bad is a 62-minute, 47-minute American television series created by Vince Gilligan that ran concurrently in the United States and Canada from 20 January 2008 to 29 September 2013. It was later released on Netflix.


The series focuses on Walter White, an overqualified chemistry professor and family man. When he is diagnosed with terminal lung cancer, he turns to crime to secure his family's financial future. With the help of a former student, Jesse Pinkman, he starts manufacturing and selling methamphetamine.



The story takes place in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Walter "Walt" White is a high school chemistry teacher. He lives in Albuquerque, New Mexico, with his disabled son and pregnant wife. The day after his 50th birthday, he was diagnosed with terminal lung cancer with an estimated life expectancy of two years. Everything fell apart because of him. He then decided to set up a lab and sell methamphetamine to give his family a comfortable financial future after his death.
