Al-Aqsa Mosque

The Al-Aqsa Mosque means the "farthest" mosque and is the largest mosque in Palestine. It was built in the seventh century in Jerusalem and is the third holiest place in Islam, after Mecca and Medina. It is the largest mosque in Jerusalem.

It is a very important and incredibly beautiful religious symbol. It is one of the most famous mosques in the world. This place is full of history, it is also the second mosque built on earth. You must see this place because the peace and quiet is extraordinary.


Despite this, the mosque has retained its historical and spiritual significance. The 14-hectare al-Aqsa Mosque encompasses the gilded Dome of the Rock, arguably one of the most iconic monuments in Jerusalem, and the al-Qibli Mosque, both of which are considered sacred.

The entire complex, bounded by the outer walls, measures 144,000 square metres and includes mosques, prayer halls, courtyards and religious monuments. The gilding of the glittering Dome of the Rock is familiar to Muslims around the world, and praying at the site is considered a great privilege.


Originally, the mosque was a simple building that rested on wooden trusses, but the structure seen today was built by the Umayyad Caliph Walid bin Abd al-Malik ibn Marwan in the early 8th century.

Throughout its history, the mosque has experienced many earthquakes and attacks, which have left their mark on the original structure. The mosque has a capacity of 5,000 worshippers and the entire esplanade can accommodate nearly 200,000 people. Between 1969 and 1983, the dome of al-Aqsa Mosque was coated with aluminium by anodising, giving it a silvery appearance. In 1983, it was restored to its original dark grey lead coating.
